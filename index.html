Fast confidence review

The relationships you should be able to explain without notes.

Relay vs. breaker

Relay: senses, compares, decides, and sends a trip/close signal.
Breaker: physically makes or interrupts the power circuit.

CT vs. PT

CT: scaled current for meters/relays.
PT/VT: scaled voltage for meters/relays.

Capacitor vs. reactor

Capacitor: counters inductive demand and supports voltage.
Reactor: adds inductance and counters capacitive voltage rise.

Induction vs. synchronous motor

Induction: rotor runs below synchronous speed so slip produces torque.
Synchronous: rotor locks to the rotating field and runs at synchronous speed.

Generator controls

Prime-mover input: mainly controls MW/real power.
Excitation: mainly controls voltage and MVAR/reactive exchange.

Transformer law

Voltage follows turns ratio. Current changes inversely. Frequency stays the same. Laminated cores reduce eddy-current heating.
